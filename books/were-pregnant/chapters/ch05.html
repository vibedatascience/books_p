The Fifth Month

The midpoint of pregnancy — the anatomy scan reveals your baby's development in detail, fetal movement becomes unmistakable, and all organs settle into place.

Weeks 18–22 Overview

By the fifth month, the pregnancy is visibly established. The belly is rounding, the baby is moving with increasing regularity, and the anatomy ultrasound — typically performed between Weeks 18 and 20 — offers the most detailed look yet at fetal development. This month also brings the option of learning the baby's sex, either through genetic testing results or the ultrasound itself. Physically, the pregnant partner is in relatively good form: energy remains stable, mood is generally positive, and appetite is healthy. The primary discomforts are mechanical — hip pain, leg cramps, round ligament pain — as the body adapts to a rapidly growing uterus.

Week 18 — Gender Determination

The baby's genitals may now be visible on ultrasound. In girls, the fallopian tubes have formed. The ears have migrated to their final position on the head. At about 5.5 inches long, the baby is beginning to look distinctly human. If genetic testing was performed a few weeks ago, results typically arrive this week, revealing both potential birth defects and fetal sex.

Cardiovascular changes are accelerating: blood volume, blood pressure, and heart rate are all shifting, which can cause drops in blood pressure and dizziness when standing quickly. Appetite is increasing, heartburn and flatulence persist, and stress about the upcoming birth may begin to surface. This is a good time for the family to finalize decisions about whether and how to share the baby's sex — privately, with close family, or via a broader announcement.

Week 19 — Sensory Development

The brain is in a period of rapid sensory development. The baby, now about the size of a mango, is covered in a white, waxy coating called vernix caseosa — a protective substance that shields the developing skin from prolonged exposure to amniotic fluid. Arms and legs have reached proportionate length relative to the body.

The expanding belly may be causing round ligament pain — sharp discomfort in the hip area or lower abdomen triggered by sudden movement. Nocturnal leg cramps become more common as the uterus puts increasing pressure on blood vessels and nerves. A maternity pillow can significantly improve sleep quality; options range from wedge-style supports to full-body U-shaped or C-shaped pillows. If the pregnant partner has not yet acquired one, this is the week to address that. Light massage of the legs and hips can help relieve cramping.

Week 20 — Halfway There

You have reached the midpoint of the 40-week pregnancy. The baby is roughly the length of a banana — about 10 inches — and all organs are now in place, functioning as miniature versions of their full-size counterparts. Gender-specific development is well underway: in girls, the uterus is formed and the ovaries contain approximately 7 million primitive eggs (this number will decline significantly by birth). In boys, the testicles have begun their descent toward the scrotum.

The uterus now sits above the belly button, and the belly has taken on a distinctly rounded shape. Swelling, particularly in the hands and feet, may become noticeable. Fetal movement is increasingly vigorous — twisting, turning, and kicking are now regular occurrences. This is a meaningful milestone worth acknowledging: half the journey is complete.

Week 21 — Coordinated Movement

The baby is approaching a foot in length and a pound in weight. The eyebrows have grown in. Arms and legs are now fully proportionate, which means movements are more coordinated — deliberate punches and kicks rather than the jerky reflexive motions of earlier weeks. The baby is swallowing amniotic fluid for both hydration and nutrition, and the flavor of that fluid varies with the mother's diet. Research suggests early flavor exposure may influence postnatal food preferences.

First-trimester complaints like nausea and dizziness have largely disappeared. Energy and mood are generally positive. However, increased oil production and rising progesterone levels can cause skin changes. Watch for signs of preeclampsia — a dangerous blood-pressure condition that can first appear around this time. Report persistent headaches, visual disturbances, or sudden swelling to your provider. Varicose and spider veins may also develop as the body produces extra blood volume; sleeping on the left side, keeping legs uncrossed, and staying physically active can help circulation.

Week 22 — Approaching Viability

The baby now weighs a full pound and is the size of a papaya. It looks unmistakably like a small infant — skinny, with translucent skin, but with all recognizable features in place. The pancreas has developed, and the irises are formed though not yet pigmented. The baby is actively grasping — including the umbilical cord — and can respond to loud sounds, bright light, and other external stimuli. In just one to two more weeks, the fetus reaches the threshold of viability outside the womb.

The pregnant partner's belly is now clearly identifiable as a pregnancy. A dark line (linea nigra) may appear running up the center of the abdomen, along with other pigmentation changes in the skin. Sleep is becoming more difficult as the baby grows; heartburn, flatulence, and general physical discomfort contribute to stress. Hot flashes and episodes of excessive sweating are common — cool showers, cold compresses, and staying well-hydrated can help. Dehydration and low blood sugar are frequent causes of dizziness; regular water intake and consistent snacking throughout the day are essential. Begin the breastfeeding conversation with your partner and provider if you have not already.

The Bottom Line

“Month five is the emotional centerpiece of pregnancy — the anatomy scan, the midpoint milestone, and the steady drumbeat of increasingly strong fetal movement make the baby feel real in a way that earlier weeks did not.”

“The body is working hard but holding steady. Stay alert for warning signs like preeclampsia, keep hydration and nutrition consistent, and take advantage of this relatively stable window to finalize plans.”
